July 1, 2026

Campaign Arc: Abyssal Anchors Crisis · Chapter: The Curse of the Hells · Date: 2026-07-01 · In-Game Year: 1836

Session 78 — The Curse of the Hells

Quick Summary

The dead of Buddleia's townhouse learn why you don't haunt a party of level 11 adventurers. Murder ghosts and their ghoul rearguard are destroyed — Ki finishes the wall-hiders by pounding the floor with thunder. Then the bill comes due: Grai has carried the Devil Prince's Curse of the Hells since the Bastion, and removing it costs her 4d10 psychic damage at the end of every long rest, forever. Camila's second family ring is missing from her unclaimed body — Blaine Kraverrogg almost certainly holds his own key to the Shadebarrow.

The Battle of Buddleia's Townhouse

  • Session opens on rolled initiative; the DM opens with an act of spousal warfare, crowd-controlling his own wife's barbarian
  • Imdra unleashes her unicorn of reverse hurting (healing spirit); Grai tears into the ghosts with the Greatsword of Wounding
  • Bohdi goes full elemental: Freedom of the Waves sends a column of water crashing through the room, then he teleports into the melee and detonates a blast of cold
  • Finn debuts the Magentan Sun-Saw — magenta, mechanical, immediately blastin'
  • The ghosts flee through the party, then through the walls, leaving ghouls — Grai murders the shit out of them
  • Ana, with one monster left standing, casts See Invisibility (finally gets to do something)
  • Bohdi Fire Bolts the babbling green thing; Finn finishes it
  • Ki pounds the floor with a thunderous blast that kills the murder ghosts through the walls. The haunting ends not with an exorcism but with a bard hitting the building until the dead stop moving

The Curse of the Hells

  • The Devil Prince (Session 74, Bastion of the Fading Dawn) left something in Grai: subtract 1d4 from every attack roll and saving throw, anchored by the "Barbs of Hell"
  • High Scholar Lyra Finch (Order of Mages, Westruun library) casts Legend Lore from an oak case of ivory strips and incense — her eyes go stark white, and Finn sees hellfire burning in them; she is out for ten minutes
  • The verdict: only Wish removes the curse cleanly. Anything lesser leaves "echoes" — the curse lifts, but 4d10 psychic damage strikes at the end of every long rest, forever
  • Grai takes the echoes. Every morning, hell gets one more swing at her — and every morning she gets up anyway
  • Meanwhile Ki has started molting, courtesy of his new cursed cloak

The Missing Ring

  • Finn questions the undertaker: Camila's body is unclaimed and the second family ring is missing
  • Her townhouse shows a noble house in slow decline since her husband's death years ago
  • Conclusion: Blaine Kraverrogg has solid directions to the Shadebarrow — and likely a key of his own. The race is no longer against his head start; it's against a man holding the same ring

Quash and the Wrecked Laboratory

  • Quash's laboratory is wrecked from experimenting on the captured abyssal anchor; he claims to be "almost done"
  • Finn attempts to talk him into carrying the anchor to Emon himself, and is, by unanimous account, not doing a convincing job

Loot & Rewards

  • None recorded. The session's ledger was paid in the other direction: Grai now owes 4d10 psychic per long rest.

Next Session Preview

Northeast to Ebonroot and the Shadebarrow: find the split-tongue monolith, cross the mud, and beat Blaine Kraverrogg to the iron gate — with Buddleia's ring in Ki's pocket and, possibly, its twin in Blaine's.

Session 77 — The Remnants Lead

Quick Summary

The party works the Remnants lead in Westruun. Zeloah promotes Bohdi into the Clasp's inner circle ("Spook"), and Fetch gives up the Remnants' location: the Shadebarrow in Ebonroot. Major Events

  • Party rests at the park; everyone gains inspiration.
  • Zeloah promotes Bohdi into the Clasp inner circle as a "Spook" (a black cloak marks the role).
  • At the Saddled Planes Cow, Sable and Kellen recount the Kimal guild war: the Clasp run out, "the Myriad won," and Fetch blamed for selling them out (Katalina Caines, Gaggle Thunderfist, Slithering Fang).
  • At the Underweave Haberdashery, Fetch reveals a forged signature framing him on Myriad letterhead and names the Remnants location: the Shadebarrow in Ebonroot, reached via Baron Halthos's bloodline (Camila, Timver, Buddleia Austin).   • The party finds the haunted Buddleia Austin, takes her cursed signet ring (the Shadebarrow's iron-gate key); learns Camila died nine days prior and the ancestors are waking at Ebonroot.
  • Off-screen: the Westruun anchor needs moving; Quash tried to disintegrate it (hole in the Cobalt Reserve floor), upsetting Griselda Cassius.

Loot & Rewards

  • Buddleia Austin's cursed signet ring (Shadebarrow key) — carried by Ki.
  • Black cloak — token of Bohdi's "Spook" promotion.
  • Inspiration for all party members.

Session 74 — The Bastion of the Fading Dawn

Previous Session Recap

Session 73 brought the party to Jorenn Village, the silver boom town with no Tal'Dorei Council presence. They met the Shade Watch and Shade Master Arhana Lewyn, learned that "complications in the Hills" are taking children, and at the Shady Tap Room picked up two leads from Mama Mara: a warning that going north means death, and a Temple of the Dawn Father up in the hills.

Session Info

  • Date: 2026-05-20
  • Campaign Arc: Abyssal Anchors Crisis
  • Players Present: Ana, Bohdi, Finn, Grai, Imdra, Ki

Quick Summary

The party goes north despite the warning, crossing 30 miles of devil-haunted wasteland to reach the Temple of the Dawn Father, the Bastion of the Fading Dawn. Inside they meet Father Abraham, who has spent 18 years holding back the infernal residue of the historic Battle of Umbral Hills. While the party searches his belongings, an explosion breaks his wards, a divine gate tears open the sky, and the Devil Prince of Indulgence descends and begins overwhelming the party.

Detailed Summary

Correction: Grai's Bracelet

A DM note here originally claimed Grai does not have a Bracelet of Blur. This has since been reversed (2026-07-01): Grai does have the Bracelet of Blur — it was crafted for her by Ana (party Artificer).

Heading North

Against the Jorenn warning, the party heads north roughly 30 miles. The land is barren, as if a titan had stomped it flat. They see numbers of fiends traveling in packs — these are devils, not demons. The terrain turns to dunes, rocky and volcanic.

The Monolith

A stark, solitary monolith of alabaster stone rises ahead, pushing back the darkness, ringed by what the notes call a "defiant garden." This is the Bastion of the Fading Dawn — the Temple of the Dawn Father that Mama Mara described.

Crossing the Threshold

Stepping inside feels like stepping out of the storm and into a sun-drenched library. A floating, honeycomb-like lattice of magic fills the space. It is markedly quiet, and the scent of burning incense drifts from the chapel.

Father Abraham

In the chapel, an older, very tan man kneels in prayer, eyes closed, beneath a crystal, bathed in a pillar of concentrated sunlight.

  • Ki tries to break his concentration and fails
  • The prayer is in a language no one recognizes
  • Ana joins him in prayer

When the priest finally stops, the chapel becomes "infested again" and the lattice quivers. He looks annoyed: "Can I help you?"

He is Father Abraham. This is a Sanctuary to the Dawn Father, the Bastion of the Fading Dawn.

What Abraham Knows

The party explains the Abyssal Anchors and the ley-lines. Abraham, in turn, lays out the history and stakes:

  • He references the Battle of Umbral Hills
  • The chapel's magic is holding back infernal residue from that battle
  • He has been here 18 years; the cathedral itself is 200+ years old
  • The fall of Drasig (a dictator) and the rise of Tal'Dorei: the Battle of Umbral Hills is when the people of the Umbral Hills defeated Drasig
  • The space between here and the Hells is thin — the place is supercharged for blood magic
  • Oz was here, and went North West

While Abraham finishes praying, the party rifles through his belongings. Contents not itemized in the notes.

The Gate Opens

An explosion breaks the protective magic; it then returns. The sky rains gold. A dark-iron spike — an enormous trident — splits the sky.

Zelthrax the Folded Promise identifies it as a divine gate.

Charcoal-black feet break through the plane. It is the Devil Prince of Indulgence.

The Fight

The party engages the Devil Prince. Per the notes, he is destroying them. The session ends mid-combat. (Continues in Session 75.)
